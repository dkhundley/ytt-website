FAQs

Do I need to be experienced with yoga?

Nope! If you have never practiced yoga before, I will offer cues along the way to help you throughout the practice. Additionally, you'll find that many of the cues repeat, so as the class goes on, you'll likely find yourself starting to get the hang of things easily.

What should I wear?

Anything that you feel comfortable moving around in. Any athletic wear is totally fine. I would personally steer clear of stiff clothes like jeans. (And be aware that you likely will sweat anyway!)

Can I bring a friend?

Yes please, I would encourage it! The more, the merrier!

How difficult is power yoga?

I like to think of power yoga as a blend between yoga as you might traditionally know it and a cardio class like HIIT. It is challenging, but the encouragement here is that you take what you need on your mat. Throughout the class, I will offer modifications to various poses to help make the experience a fulfilling one for you.

What if I don't have a mat?

I have around 3-4 extra mats that people can borrow, and I'd be glad to have those cleaned if you would like to use one. Now, if you plan to roll up with a crew of 5+ people who all need mats, I unfortunately won't be able to provide a mat for everybody. But you don't necessarily need a formal mat! A large beach towel would also be okay.

Are the practice teaching courses directly sponsored by Yoga Lab?

No. While I took my yoga teacher training through Yoga Lab and will host a few classes at their facility, my practice classes are not directly sponsored by Yoga Lab.
